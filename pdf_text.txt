1 SCOPE 
1.1 Purpose 
The purpose of this document is to establish guidelines and describe critical roles 
supporting the Bidding and Project execution phases. During the transition period 
(see. ┬º1.3 Effective Date), this document ensures consistency and compliance on how 
the Railway Systems Business Unit (hereinafter RSBU or the Company) allocates 
responsibilities and functional relationships for key roles within the teams operating 
in Bidding and Project execution phases. It also defines the relationship between the 
functional and team organisations.

4 TRANSITION RULES  
4.1 Organisational Principles 
4.1.1  Organisational Key Dimension  
For the purpose of Bidding and Project execution, RSBU operates through a two -
dimensional matrix structure, combining: 
ΓÇó the functional organisation: grouping specific organisational functions with job 
specific accountabilities and competences; 
ΓÇó the bidding team organi sation or the project team organisation: aimed at 
ensuring effective and efficient delivery of Bidding and Project execution 
phases. 
4.1.2  Functional Organisation  
The functional organisation is mapped in RSBU Organisational Chart ( published on 
COSMO/COSMIC).  
The functional organisation ensures: 
ΓÇó standardisation of processes, products (where applicable) and competences by 
ensuring the use of common procedures and tools; 
ΓÇó resources staffing on bids and projects; 
ΓÇó workload and capacity planning; 
ΓÇó development of the resourceΓÇÖs competences; 
ΓÇó capitalisation and sharing of the know-how. 
The details of the key accountabilities assigned to the CompanyΓÇÖs organisational 
functions are described in Roles and Mandates/functional job descriptions. 
The operative bridge between the functional organisation and the business delivery is 
the Project Team Organisation ( PTO), established to achieve specific bid or project 
objectives and in charge of the bidding or project activities. 
4.1.3  Bidding Team Operating Model and Project Execution Team 
Operating Model  
Bidding Teams and Project Teams are the key pillars of RSBU delivery processes. 
Collaboration, cross-functional resourcing and a process approach are the key success 
factors in the deployment of RSBUΓÇÖs business strategy, as no individual or single 
organisational function holds all the required levers needed to achieve the business 
objectives. 
Integrated project teams effectively staffed with required skills, competences and 
level of resourcing are the key drivers needed to achieve Company goals.

Each Bidding or Project Team is led by a single accountable leader, respectively the 
Bid Manager (BM) during the bidding phase and the Project Manager (PM) during the 
project execution phase, who holds overall accountability. 
The BM/PM is ultimately accountable for the staffing of the overall team organisation, 
with their direct reporting team members (such as PE) responsible for the staffing of 
their subordinate project structure. Based on Scope of Work (SoW) and requirements, 
the BM/PM is accountable for providing staffing requirements to the functional 
organisation. The functional organisation is accountable for allocating the most 
appropriate profiles, to be agreed with the BM/PM. 
Depending on the Scope of Work (SoW) and requirements highlighted by the Bid 
Manager or Project Manager and communicated to the relevant  functional 
organisation, the direct reporting Team Members are proposed by the functional 
managers from the appropriate functions within the Organisation and agreed with the 
Bid/Project Manager . The functional organisation analyses the Bid/Project 
requirements with the Bid or Project Manager and allocates the most appropriate 
profiles, ensuring correct allocation of skills and workload.  
Whilst Team Members are identified from their relevant functions, they are managed 
by the Bid Manager or Project Manager (potentially via other managers within the Bid 
or Project), and are accountable for the delivery of their SoW, including all associated 
responsibilities in term of scope, schedule, cost, quality and risks relevant to Bidding 
or Project objectives. 
Team Members are required to conduct their activities in accordance with relevant 
RSBU processes. 
4.1.4  Work Breakdown Structure and Accountability Model  
The Bid or Project SoW is structured through a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), a 
hierarchical decomposition of a project into smaller, manageable components called 
work packages. The levels of breakdown may vary on the nature and complexity of 
the Bid or Project, with the lowest level defined as a Work Package (representing the 
level at which scope, schedule, cost, deliverables and performance can be planned, 
estimated, monitored, controlled, and delivered). 
The structure of the Bidding or Project team will reflect the structure of the WBS, with 
four levels of role envisioned: 
1. Bid/Project Manager: Overall accountable for the Bid or Project delivery  
2. Main Work Package Manager (MWPM): Level 1 reporting to the Bid or 
Project Manager, accountable for the first level of breakdown of the WBS (often 
related to an overall function such as Engineering) 
3. Work Package Manager (WPM):  Level 2 or lower to the Bid or Project 
Manager, reporting to a MWPM or another WPM with accountability for a section 
of the WBS and the management of subordinate work packages

4.1.5  Issue Resolution and Escalation Principles  
The Bid or Project Manager and the MWPMs, WPMs, and WPLs shall endeavour to 
resolve bidding or project execution issues internally before escalating them to the 
relevant functional organisation. Issues shall be escalated to the relevant functional 
organisation, as appropriate, when the required know-how is not available at Bidding 
or Project execution level, or when there is a reasonable risk of the team being unable 
to maintain control of delivery in line with targets.  
In order to ensure seamless transition between the bidding and the project execution 
phase, the team should pursue continuity of individuals allocated to key roles (where 
possible). Additionally, Bidding team members should strongly support the Project 
Team during the initial phases of Project execution (PJM-01 Project Launch and PJM-
02 Project Planning) in alignment with the relevant functional organisation.  
Where alignment between the functional and project organisation (regarding staffing 
or tailoring) cannot be achieved in due time, the matter shall be escalated to the 
appropriate management level for resolution. 
4.1.6  Team Tailoring and Governance  
The Team Organisations described in this document represent the standard 
composition of the Bid or Project team and shall apply as the reference model. 
It is the accountability of the Bid/Project Manager to ensure the alignment of the 
Bid/Project Team Organisation with the standard roles described in this document.  
Only where formally justified by specific characteristics, contract requirements, or 
requirements of the Bid, Project, or Business, the Bid or Project Manager may tailor

4.2 Bidding Team 
4.2.1  Bidding Team ΓÇô Standard Principles  
The Bidding Team  is the inter -functional organisational structure accountable for 
developing and managing the bid, under the leadership of the Bid Manager, until its 
closing.  
A Bidding Team is established to pursue new orders whenever the RSBU takes part in 
a commercial opportunity, such as public tenders, unsolicited proposals, or direct 
negotiations, following a ΓÇÿGoΓÇÖ decision and requiring a full bidding process. 
 
Bidding Team Members are staffed by the relevant functional organisation based on 
the scope of work and the specific tender requirements and competences requested 
by the Bid Manager (BM). The composition of the Bidding Team shall be defined and 
aligned with the structure outlined in this document and may be tailored ΓÇöwhere 
justified by bid characteristics, customer requirements or specific requirementsΓÇöin 
alignment with the functional organisation. 
Although the Bidding Team is expected to be staffed in accordance with this 
document, due to the variety in bid size and complexity, the following principles may 
be taken in consideration for tailoring: 
ΓÇó A Bidding Team role does not necessarily correspond to 1 Full Time Equivalent 
(FTE). Based on workload and complexity, the Bid Manager and the relevant 
functional organisation determine the appropriate FTE allocation  
ΓÇó In certain cases (e.g., small and less complex bids with highly experienced 
professionals), one individual may cover more than one role  (either fully or 
partially), ensuring appropriate reporting within the functional organisation.  
As key principles: 
ΓÇó A single Capture Leader is appointed for each Opportunity and is accountable 
for its commercial and strategic success, working closely with the Bid Manager 
throughout bid preparation. 
ΓÇó A single Bid Manager is appointed for each bid and leads the Bidding Team 
across all business segments, regions and legal entities, holding full end-to-end 
accountability for the bid, ensuring integrated technical, commercial, financial 
and governance robustness. 
ΓÇó A single Project Engineer acts as the sole technical lead on the bid solution, 
ensuring end-to-end technical coherence, integrity and feasibility. 
ΓÇó The BM and PE can be supported by Level -2 Bid Team roles in line with the 
WBS (e.g. WPM, WPL), subordinate within the bid hierarchy.

4.2.2  Bidding Team ΓÇô Roles specific to the Bidding Phase  
4.2.2.1 Capture Leader 
The Capture Leader is the strategic and commercial leader of the Bids. The Capture 
Leader role is performed, as required, by the Sales Manager or by other roles 
designated for the specific bid. In the case of VHC, the role of Capture Leader can be 
executed by the Bid Manager. 
The Capture Leader is fully accountable end-to end for the overall success of the 
Bid - from defining the capture strategy, through securing internal approvals, to 
submitting the Offer, and then managing negotiations and finalising the contract.  
In LoB SRS, the Capture Leader is accountable, with the support of the Bid Manager, 
for the internal approval process according to the RSBU governance rules.

Following the approval and authorization stages, and based on the mandate received, 
the Capture Leader is responsible for leading the negotiations and for finalising the 
best possible contract for RSBU. 
4.2.2.2 Bid Manager  
The Bid Manager (BM) is accountable for the end-to-end preparation of the technical

4.3 Project Execution Team 
4.3.1  Project Execution Team ΓÇô Standard Principles  
The Project Execution Team (hereafter referred to as Project Team ) is the inter -
functional organisational structure responsible for managing the Project under the 
leadership of the Project Manager, during the whole project lifecycle. A Project Team 
is set up when a new contract is awarded to RSBU and a project has to be launched 
in order to execute the relevant scope of work.  
The composition of the Project Team shall be defined and aligned with the structure 
outlined in this document. In case of specific customer requests, a different additional 
team representation can be created specifically for external use only, with no impact 
on the way the RSBU manages and delivers the project. 
Although the Project Team is expected to be staffed in accordance with this document, 
due to the variety in size and complexity of project execution, the following principles 
may be taken in consideration for tailoring: 
ΓÇó A Project Team role does not necessarily correspond to 1 Full Time Equivalent 
(FTE). Based on workload and complexity, the Project Manager and the relevant 
functional organisation determine the appropriate FTE allocation  
ΓÇó In certain cases (e.g. small and less complex projects with highly experienced 
professionals), one individual may cover more than one role  (either fully or 
partially), subject to joint assessment by the Project Manager and the relevant 
functional organisation. In such cases, the individual ensures appropriate 
functional reporting within the functional organisations 
Project Team Members are responsible for performing the assigned activities on 
budget, quality and on time, and in line with Safety > > Quality > Delivery > Cost 
(S>>Q>D>C) approach, according to the relevant plans/schedule and to the project 
baseline. 
The Project Team Members are responsible for the management and control of the 
assigned project activities within RSBU  and are delegated full authority by the 
organisation to do this within the project context. 
In addition to its role-specific responsibility, each Project Team Member is responsible 
to: 
ΓÇó issue all the documentation relevant to their functional area 
ΓÇó provide the Project Planner and Project Manager with the related detailed 
project delivery schedule and periodical physical progress 
ΓÇó provide to the Project Controller (via any applicable tool or system) a periodical 
progress of the hours, costs and requirements related to the execution of the 
activities within the assigned SoW / WPs 
ΓÇó provide the Project Manager with the evaluation of the risks and opportunities 
related to the assigned SoW

6 COMPARISON SUMMARY  
A comparison between the former RSBU and legacy GTS Bidding and Project Team 
Organisations has been performed. Following this analysis, the following main points 
of difference and similarity are noted: 
ΓÇó Both legacy businesses operate in a project environment and have a number 
of established  standard roles that are staff ed in case of  Bidding or Project 
execution. 
ΓÇó Both legacy businesses require collaboration between a Bidding or Project 
organisation and a function organisation in order to meet the objectives of the 
business. 
ΓÇó Differences exist in the legacy organisations in the specific roles required as
